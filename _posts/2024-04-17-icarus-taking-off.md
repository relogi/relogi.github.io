Dusty Farr is a hero.

It’s easy to do the right thing when you’ve been surrounded by good people all your life. It's easy to do the right thing when the posters on the wall of your life are positive, life-affirming and forgiving of mistakes. It’s easy to live a saint's life living on a mountian top isolated from the endless mire of choicethat is modern life. Modernity just might be defined as drownding in choice.

Dusty Farr is a christian bigot raising a young trans girl. He spent years, most of her life, in denial of his child’s humanity as she fell deeper and deeper into crisis. Highschool triggered self-harm and attempts at suicide, a sure-fire recipie for tragedy. And yet, somehow… not.
 
One day Dusty Farr looked down at the child hurting in his arms and saw his daughter. Saw the daughter he always had. Saw the evil in himself. And somehow found the strength to love… found the strength to ask his child, what can we do now.

To fight the fight as hard as you did, to triumph over yourself is a thing way too few of our cultural heros ever manage. Most of them don’t even get close to that kind of courage. I raise a glass of humble and awe and joy... bottoms up… if I find half the strength of your heart I will live a good life.
